My Experience at PhonePe.

This is a short blog post about my experience at Phonepe and the projects that I worked on during my tenure there.

Anomaly Detection Project

The requirement for this project came out of an actual system failure that costed the company millions of dollars. The surface-level view of what the Phonepe app does is, that it accepts money from one entity and transfers it to another entity - i.e it is a payment gateway (something like Paypal). Basically, there was a synchronization issue between the microservices due to which the system was refunding payments to senders and also transferring money to recipients. This happened for about 45min and there was no system in place to detect this outage. The Anomaly Detection System was a project initiated by the company to make sure that such anomalies are detected in the future thus preventing potential loss. The progress of the project was tracked by the Founder/CTO of the company - Rahul Chari. I exceeded the expectations of my managers and was appreciated highly for it. What was initially supposed to be an alerting system for monitoring approximately 5 metrics pointed out by the business team turned out to develop into a much more sophisticated system monitoring more than 21 metrics which could cause potential loss to the Company. The system could be used to detect breaches in hardcoded thresholds as well as relative changes in a particular metric. I became known to have worked on that project not only in my team but in teams across different branches. Would love to discuss this in detail if possible.
